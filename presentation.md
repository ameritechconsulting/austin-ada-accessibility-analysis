## SLIDE 6-7: Curb Ramp Conditions
### Essential for Crosswalk Accessibility

#### Current Status
- **Curb Ramps Present**: 83.3% of surveyed crosswalks
- **Functional Curb Ramps**: 83.3% in good/excellent condition
- **Deteriorated/Missing**: 16.7% requiring repair or installation

#### Common Issues Identified
| Issue Type | Frequency | Severity |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| Missing curb ramps | High | Critical |
| Cracked/broken surfaces | High | High |
| Improper slope/grade | Medium | High |
| Obstructed by vegetation | Medium | Medium |
| Snow/ice accumulation zones | Medium | Medium |
| Inadequate drainage | Low | Medium |

#### Maintenance Priorities
1. **Immediate**: Missing or severely damaged ramps at high-traffic intersections
2. **30 Days**: Cracked surfaces creating trip hazards
3. **90 Days**: Slope corrections and drainage improvements

## SLIDE 12: Policy Considerations

### Recommendations Framework

2. **Compliance Tracking System**
   - Deploy digital asset inventory and monitoring system
   - Establish quarterly reporting to City Council
   - Create public-facing accessibility dashboard

3. **Stakeholder Coordination**
   - Convene advisory committee (accessibility advocates, mobility services, city departments)
   - Monthly implementation meetings
   - Quarterly public progress reports

#### Short-Term Improvements (3-12 months)
1. **Targeted Infrastructure Improvements**
   - Address 27.9% of non-compliant crosswalks
   - Focus: curb ramps, signal accessibility, surface conditions
   - Budget allocation: $506.3M for FY[XX]

2. **Enhanced Maintenance Program**
   - Quarterly preventive inspections

3. **Community Engagement**
   - Public accessibility workshops
   - Accessibility hotline/reporting system
   - Neighborhood-specific improvement plans

#### Medium-Term Strategy (1-2 years)
1. **Universal Design Integration**
   - Incorporate accessibility into all new/retrofit projects
   - Green infrastructure with accessibility requirements
   - Smart signal technology with multi-modal accessibility

2. **Funding Diversification**
   - Federal grants (USDOT, HUD)
   - State matching funds
   - Public-Private Partnerships
   - Bond initiatives

   - Zero critical violations at schools/healthcare/transit hubs

   - Smart accessibility features (pedestrian countdown signals, wayfinding)
   - Weather-adaptive infrastructure
   - Integration with transit accessibility improvements

3. **Data-Driven Governance**

   - Predictive maintenance modeling
   - Equity-focused resource allocation
