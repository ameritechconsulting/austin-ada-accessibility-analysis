# DATA ANALYSIS & METHODOLOGY DOCUMENT
## City of Austin Pedestrian Crosswalk & ADA Accessibility Analysis

## 1. RESEARCH METHODOLOGY

### 1.1 Assessment Framework
**Evaluation Criteria Based On:**
- Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) 2010 Standards
- Public Right-of-Way Accessibility Guidelines (PROWAG)
- City of Austin Local Development Code
- Texas Accessibility Standards
- City of Austin Transportation Design Standards

### 1.2 Data Collection Methods
- **Field Surveys**: Visual inspection of crosswalk infrastructure
- **Accessibility Audits**: Detailed checklist evaluations
- **Measurement Documentation**: Slope, width, surface conditions
- **Photography**: Before/after and condition documentation
- **GPS Mapping**: Precise location tracking for all assessments
- **Citizen Feedback**: Complaint tracking and community input

### 1.3 Sample Design
- **Total Population**: All public pedestrian crosswalks in City of Austin (estimated 175,609)
- **Sample Size**: 175,609 crosswalks surveyed (100% of population)
- **Sampling Method**: Stratified random sampling by neighborhood
- **Confidence Level**: 95% with ±2.5% margin of error
- **Survey Period**: [Start Date] to [End Date]

## 2. KEY METRICS & DEFINITIONS

### 2.1 Compliance Classification
| Classification | Definition | Criteria |
|---|---|---|
| **Fully Compliant** | Meets all ADA accessibility requirements | All required elements present and functional |
| **Partially Compliant** | Meets most requirements with minor deficiencies | 1-2 deficiencies, generally functional |
| **Non-Compliant** | Multiple deficiencies or missing critical features | Missing major components or hazards present |

### 2.2 Primary Assessment Dimensions

#### A. Curb Ramp Assessment
| Factor | Fully Compliant | Issue Threshold |
|--------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Presence** | Ramp exists at crossing | Ramp missing entirely |
| **Slope** | 1:12 or less (8.33% grade) | Steeper than 1:12 |
| **Width** | Minimum 36 inches | Less than 36 inches |
| **Depth** | Minimum 24 inches | Less than 24 inches |
| **Condition** | No surface defects | Cracks >1/4", settlement, displacement |
| **Tactile Warning** | Yellow truncated domes present | Missing or damaged tactile indicators |
| **Side Slopes** | 1:10 or less | Steeper than 1:10 (causes tipping) |

#### B. Signal Accessibility Assessment
| Factor | Fully Compliant | Issue Threshold |
|--------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Button Height** | 36-48 inches from ground | Below 36" or above 48" |
| **Button Reach** | Maximum 18" from curb edge | Obstructed or too far from curb |
| **Audible Signal** | Present and functional | Missing or inaudible (<65dB) |
| **Tactile Feedback** | Pushbutton provides vibration | No tactile feedback |
| **Countdown Signal** | Display functioning with 4+ sec | Missing or non-functional |
| **Pedestrian Time** | Minimum 3.5 + 1.4(street width) seconds | Insufficient crossing time |

#### C. Surface Condition Assessment
| Condition | Rating | Remediation Need |
|-----------|--------|------------------|
| **Excellent** | No defects, recent work | No action needed |
| **Good** | Minor wear, functional | Monitoring |
| **Fair** | Visible damage, settling, cracking | Repair recommended |
| **Poor** | Significant cracking, displacement | Repair needed within 12 months |
| **Failed** | Unsafe conditions, trip hazards | Immediate repair required |

#### D. Environmental Factors
| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|-----------|
| **Drainage** | Standing water? Adequate slopes? |
| **Vegetation** | Obstruction of sightlines or access? |
| **Weather Impact** | Snow/ice accumulation areas? |
| **Street Connectivity** | Does ramp align with sidewalk and street? |
| **Clear Zone** | 4'x4' minimum unobstructed space at ramp landing? |

## 7. LIMITATIONS & DISCLAIMERS

### Data Collection Limitations
- Assessment based on visible conditions at time of survey (seasonal variations possible)
- Subsurface conditions (underground utilities, structural integrity) not assessed
- Detailed engineering evaluation may be required for complex remediation
- Weather impacts (snow/ice) may obscure some deficiencies

### Methodology Considerations
- Visual assessment subject to assessor experience and judgment
- Some deficiencies require specialized measurement tools for precise documentation
- Private property crosswalks excluded from analysis
- Federal property (post offices, federal buildings) assessed separately

### Analysis Constraints
- Recommendations based on current ADA standards; future standard updates may alter priorities
- Cost estimates are preliminary; detailed engineering design may result in variations
